Exposure Triangle

Aperture

Controls how much light enters the lens. Lower f‑numbers = wider aperture & shallower depth of field; higher f‑numbers = deeper focus.

Shutter Speed

Determines how long the shutter stays open. Fast speeds (e.g. 1/1000 s) freeze action; slow speeds (e.g. 1/30 s) create motion blur.

ISO

Camera’s sensitivity to light. Higher ISO lets you shoot in darker conditions but increases image noise.
